Capstone Project Plan

Introduction

Welcome to the first exercise of your Capstone Project! This project is a chance for you to show what you have learned while at Devmountain. In this exercise, you’ll spend some time generating ideas for what to build for your capstone project.

Setup

  1. Create a new Github repository called “project-ideas”.

  2. On your machine, create a folder of the same name and a new file called ideas.txt inside. Connect this to your GitHub repo.

Brainstorm

  1. Make a list of websites that you might want to test. Think of websites you visit often or websites similar to those that you have tested throughout the program. Look up some of your favorite brand’s websites. Find new websites related to one of your hobbies. Try to get a list of at least 10 websites.

  2. Once you have about 10 websites, list them in order with the one you’re most interested in testing at the top.

Finding Selectors

  1. Starting at the top, go through your list of sites and look for selectors for UI elements. If it’s proving difficult to find them, you can move on to the next site in your list. You will need to choose 2 websites for your final project.

  2. When you have found the 2 websites you’ll focus on, take note of them in your ideas file.

Major Features

  1. Make a list of major features to be tested for both of your chosen websites.

Turn In Your Work

Add and commit ideas.txt to your project-ideas Github repository. Make sure that the repo is marked as “public” or shared directly with staff.

Next Steps

Work on this over the course of the next few days. When instructed, you’ll submit your websites, selectors, features, and a test plan (test plan instructions will be given next week) to the gauntlet channel in Discord.
